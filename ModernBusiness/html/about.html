I served Spanish speaking in the California Fresno Mission

Read the story

It all started with my original call...

I was first called to serve in the Bolivia Santa Cruz North Mission. When I was in the MTC, the Bolivian government became very unstable. I received a "temporary" reassignment to Fresno, California.

Then came COVID-19

In March of 2020 I had my visa and flight plans that would take me to Bolivia. However, on March 15th, my birthday, was the first we heard of things closing down for COVID.

Receiving my official reassignment...

About two months before finishing my mission, I received an email stating that I had officially been reassigned to the California Fresno Mission. At that point it was obvious right? I am not going to pretend like it wasn't hard serving in a place I was not expecting to serve in the slightest. I definitely was caught up in the idea of serving somewhere cool. However, while my mission was nothing that I expected, it was everything I needed.

Things I learned on my mission, the things I really needed.

  1. God is aware of me.
  2. People are so deserving of love.
  3. It feels good to work hard.
  4. Spanish is an awesome language.
  5. I love biking.
  6. I love California.
  7. Mexican food is where it's at.
  8. Paleterias are also where it's at.
  9. Communication is incredibly important.
  10. The gospel makes me happy.

Most of all, I learned that people are why I needed to be in Fresno. Here is a list of the people who I am now blessed to know...

  • My companions
  • Other missionaries
  • People I taught
  • Members I worked with
  • All the dogs that I pet
  • My mission leaders
  • My mission presidents and their wives
  • And all the other lovely people in between
